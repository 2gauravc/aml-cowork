# Discovery Sprint

## Objective

The objective of this Discovery Sprint is to demonstrate a new way of creating a digital workforce.

Using KYC as the business context, the sprint will test if digital workers (agents) can be created faster with a fundamentally different operating model than traditional technology delivery. Rather than treating this as another technology project, the sprint  will evaluate how we can create, maintain and improve digital workers at a radically faster pace - using the modern method of co-creating agents with AI.

The outcome of the Discovery Sprint is more than a KYC agent Proof of Concept. It is a blueprint for how the bank can build and scale a digital workforce. If successful, the new blueprint can be extended beyond KYC - to transform knowledge work across the enterprise.

## Transformation Pillars 

The Discovery Sprint is anchored on two transformation pillars.

### 1. New technology delivery model for creating AI digital workforce (Shift Left)

Today's delivery model relies on sequential handoffs between business users, business analysts, technology and testing teams. While this model has been effective for building workflow systems, co-creating with AI is a radically different way of building digital workers—one that is faster, more iterative and closer to the expert and ultimate beneficiary - the knowledge worker. 

The sprint will test a **Shift Left** model where small teams, led by the knoweldge worker, co-creat digital workers. This dramatically reduces translation layers, shortens the feedbak loop and time to market. 

In this model specialized technology and data teams do not deliver indivisual initiatives. rather the specialized teams focus on building: 
a) Shared co-work platform 
b) Core capabilities as APIs 
c) Automated AI evaluation frameworks 
d) Autoamted testing 

The objective is not simply to deliver faster, but to enable the organisation to create many digital workers in parallel rather than a few large technology initiatives in a year.

### 2. The New Role - Knowledge Worker of the future

The new knwoledge worker does not provide user requirements and test system features. The knwoledge worker of the future is an active creator, tariner and supervisor of the digital workforce. They encode business knowledge, improve digital worker capabilities and provide human judgement where it adds the greatest value. 


## Success Criteria

The Discovery Sprint will be considered successful if it demonstrates the following:

### 1. Shift Left Delivery Model

Demonstrate that small teams of 2-4 people, with the KYC CORE analyst at the forefront, can create a meaningful KYC digital worker using an Agent Studio Platform in a short time window of 1-2 weeks. The team will not rely on traditional SDLC process or specialist technology / data expertise. 


### 3. Human-by-Exception Decision Model

Demonstrate that digital workers can perform routine KYC activities, enabling humans to focus on judgement, exception handling and on improving the digital workers. 

## Proof Scenarios 

### 1. Customer Business Profile: 
Create or update a customer business profile and produce outcome in a pre-defined format (JSON). This includes company details (UEN, incorporation date, incorporation country etc.), listing status, industry classification, PBA write-up and shell Risk assessment (PUC).

### 2. Ownership & Control: 
Create or update company ownership structure and produce outcome in pre-defined format. Includes recursive ownership unwrapping, UBO identification, RP identification and details capture, nominee arrangement assessment and complex structure assessment. 

### 3. Account Activity Review: 
Evaluate if the customer's transaction profile and counterparties are plausible given the stated business. Highlight risk if any. Produce output in a pre-defined format. 

### 4. Independent Challenge (Checker / Risk Review)
Provide independent challenge by identifying errors, omissions, inconsistencies and unsupported conslusions. Identify need for more docs / info to corroborate the conclusions and to meet policy objectives.
